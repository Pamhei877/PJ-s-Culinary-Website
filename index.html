Singju

Singju is a beloved traditional salad from Manipur, known for its unique mix of flavors and textures. It is a refreshing and nutritious dish that highlights the vibrant culture and culinary heritage of the region.

Mangal Ooti

Mangal Ooti is a traditional Manipuri dish made primarily from green peas or yellow lentils (depending on the variant) and flavored with local spices and herbs. It is a simple yet wholesome dish that is often enjoyed with rice as part of daily meals in Manipur.

Eromba

Eromba is one of the most iconic and beloved dishes of Manipur, showcasing the simplicity and richness of its traditional cuisine. It is a flavorful mash of boiled vegetables and fermented fish (ngari), spiced up with chilies and aromatic herbs. Eromba is a must-try for anyone wanting to explore the authentic flavors of Manipur.

View Recipe

Chagempomba

Chagempomba is a hearty and wholesome traditional Manipuri dish, often prepared with a variety of vegetables, herbs, and fermented ingredients. Its name derives from “Chagem,” meaning fermented soya bean (hawaijar), and “Pomba,” meaning a dish or stew. This dish showcases Manipuri cuisine's reliance on fresh, seasonal produce and fermented flavors.

Sagolhawai Ooti

Sagolhawai Ooti is a traditional Manipuri dish that is both simple and nutritious, made using horse gram (sagolhawai), a lentil variety popular for its earthy flavor and health benefits. It is a warming and flavorful dish, typically served with steamed rice, showcasing the Manipuri love for lentil-based recipes.

Kelli Chana

Kelli Chana is a popular Manipuri street food with a fascinating backstory. It is a spicy and tangy dish made with black chickpeas (chana), flavored with aromatic spices and fresh herbs. The dish is named after a woman, Kelli, who originally sold it under a tree, making it an iconic part of Manipuri food culture.

Paknam

Paknam is a beloved Manipuri dish that is both simple and delicious. It is essentially a steamed cake made with gram flour (besan) or ground rice, mixed with vegetables, herbs, and the unique flavor of fermented fish (ngari). Paknam is often wrapped in banana leaves before steaming, giving it a distinct aroma and flavor.

Pakora Thongba

Pakora Thongba is a delightful Manipuri dish that combines crispy pakoras (fried fritters) with a flavorful, mildly spiced curry. It is a great example of how Manipuri cuisine integrates texture and taste, making it a comforting and satisfying dish often served during festivals or family gatherings.

Pangkhokla Ooti

Pangkhokla Ooti is another traditional Manipuri dish, but it is less widely known compared to staples like Eromba or Singju. This dish typically features a creamy, spiced preparation of Pangkhokla, a variety of green lentils or dried peas, paired with local Manipuri flavors.

Kangsoi

Kangsoi, also known as Manipuri vegetable stew, is a light and healthy dish that embodies the simplicity of Manipuri cuisine. Made with fresh seasonal vegetables, herbs, and minimal spices, it is a clear, broth-like preparation often enjoyed as a comfort food. The dish relies on the natural flavors of the ingredients, enhanced by fermented fish (ngari) or dried fish for an authentic touch.

Chakhao Kheer

Chakhao Kheer is a traditional Manipuri dessert made from black rice (called Chakhao in Manipuri), a highly nutritious and aromatic rice variety. This dish is often prepared during festivals, celebrations, or as a special treat. The rich, slightly sweet flavor of Chakhao Kheer is complemented by the earthy flavor of black rice, making it a unique and delicious dessert in Manipuri cuisine.

Pan Ga Hawaijar Thongba

Pan Ga Hawaijar Thongba is a traditional Manipuri dish that is both flavorful and hearty, made with pan-ga (a type of fish, often freshwater fish like ngamo or sang-phi) and hawaijar (fermented soybean paste). This dish is typically enjoyed with steamed rice and is known for its bold, savory flavors, combining the earthy taste of fermented soybeans with the delicate, tender fish. It reflects the authentic taste of Manipuri cuisine, often served during festive occasions or family gatherings.
